Alcohol consumption in Ireland

Alcohol use is a leading cause of death and disability Ireland and has been identified as one of the ten leading risk factors for the burden of disease. Alcohol use is associated with numerous harmful health and social consequences, including an increased risk of a range of cancers, stroke and liver cirrhosis. Alcohol also contributes to death and disability through accidents and injuries, assault, violence, homicide and suicide.


Some people drink alcohol to relieve feelings of sadness, loneliness, stress or worry. While alcohol may make you feel better for a little while, it is not a healthy way to cope. The feelings of anxiety and depression will come back again, often worse than before.

Warning signs of becoming Alcohol dependent

Warning signs of alcoholism include the consumption of increasing amounts of alcohol and frequent intoxication, preoccupation with drinking to the exclusion of other activities, promises to quit drinking and failure to keep those promises, the inability to remember what was said or done while drinking (known as "blackouts"), personality changes associated with drinking, denial or the making of excuses for drinking, the refusal to admit excessive drinking, dysfunction or other problems at work or school, the loss of interest in personal appearance or hygiene, marital and economic problems, and the complaint of poor health, with loss of appetite, respiratory infections, or increased anxiety.

How to make a Change from Alcohol use problems

In the early stages, there may be many times when you want to use alcohol or drugs.

You may doubt your decision to change, especially if it has been a big part of your life for a long time.

Take it one day at a time. As time goes on, the cravings will get less intense and things should get a little easier.

Reducing temptation

Giving up or cutting down takes willpower. You can get through times when you have the urge to use alcohol or drugs.

To make it easier:

  • limit your access to alcohol or drugs at home
  • avoid social situations where you would normally drink or take drugs
  • keep away from people who might try to encourage you to drink or take drugs

Remind yourself why you decided to stop

Reminding yourself the reason you are wnting to change can help stop cravings of use Alcohol again. But just stopping and thinking before you do something you think you'll regret can make all the difference. Think about the way you felt and the problems you had before you made the change.

Think about how you would feel after you give in to the urge.

If you have decided to stop drinking or taking drugs, remind yourself of the reasons for this.

It might be helpful to write down the pros and cons of making a change and not making a change.

What can you do to help yourself or a loved one with a gambling problem?

  • Setting limits for yourself is one way to decrease the impact of gambling. Know when enough is enough, count your losses and go home.

  • Many betting shops allow self exclusion. If you find it difficult to stay out of the bookies, ask the workers behind the counter to exclude you from their shops. This will mean that they will deny you entry into their shop for up to six months.
